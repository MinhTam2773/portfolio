What can the chatbot answer?

Question asked: "Can you explain what RAG means?" (an off-topic general question)

A

Portfolio-focused only

"I'm here specifically to answer questions about Tam — his projects, skills, experience, and background. For general tech topics, a quick Google search would serve you better! Is there something about Tam's work I can help with?"

Stays strictly on-topic. Redirects gracefully. Keeps the chatbot's purpose laser-focused.

B

Portfolio-first, general fallback

"RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) is a technique where an AI retrieves relevant documents before generating a response — rather than relying purely on its training data. Interestingly, Tam used a multi-namespace RAG architecture in Pathr, his education platform, to retrieve program data across Alberta institutions. Want to know more about that project?"

Answers the question, then ties it back to Tam's work. Keeps visitors engaged and showcases expertise naturally.

C

Strictly gated

"I can only answer questions about Tam's portfolio. Try asking about his projects, tech stack, education, or how to contact him!"

Hard stop. No flexibility. Simple to implement but can feel robotic when a visitor asks something reasonable.
